Pentium 4 Luggable

CPU: Pentium 4 (Prescott)

RAM: 1gb RDRAM

GPU: XGI Volari V5

OS: Windows XP

One of the strangest computers in my collection, this used to be a "DJ computer". I bought this from the guy who made the DJ software for it for $60. This has been, all in all, one of my most problematic machines.

Originally, the machine came equipeed with a Ge Force MX 4400, shitty Intel motherboard, a whopping 1gb of ram, a 300 or so gb hard drive, and a keaboard with some pretty dirty alps black switches.

Both the motherboard and gpu had a insane ammount of bad caps (curse you capacitor plauge), and although I brought the mobo back, the gpu was dead for good. After that, the hard drive died and was replaced. The computer also originally had only 1 cd rom drive, leaving an ugly hole in the side of the machine, so I added another one just to plug up the hole.

One of the greatest tragedies of my collection is that this computer was origially quiped with a touch-screen kit, that has since died leaving me to use the shitty trackapd :(

One day, I found a Volari V5 sitting in a thrift store, it was a really facinating card, made by a company that only lasted a couple years before going out. I was interested and despite the reported lackluster preformance I decided to get the card. After a quick re-cap, there was no better place to put such an odd card, than into my strangest computer.
